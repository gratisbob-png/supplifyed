## THE PHILOSOPHY

**Neutrality is not the absence of value. Neutrality IS the value.**

Most comparison sites are thinly-veiled affiliate farms dressed as reviews. People know it.
A facts-only platform with clear source provenance is rare. That rarity is the moat.

The platform holds one position on every product, every ingredient, every claim:

- Here is what is in it
- Here is what the evidence says
- Here is who is saying what
- Here is where to buy it
- You decide

No winner declared. No recommendation made. No opinion offered. Ever.

### The Legal and Commercial Shield

The no-opinion rule is not just a brand position.
It is the legal and commercial foundation of the platform.

You are not reviewing. Not recommending. Not selling.
You are providing a structured showcase of publicly available and manufacturer-supplied information.
The conversion links are a convenience. Not an endorsement.
Hold this line absolutely. It protects everything.

## THE PRODUCT

### The Entry Point

Users do not arrive at a product. They arrive at an ingredient or a question.

`melatonin` — `magnesium` — `does creatine work` — `what are electrolytes`

The ingredient page answers the question empirically first. What it is. What the peer-reviewed evidence says at what doses. What it does not show. Who funded what. Then — here are the products that contain it, across three tiers, with the raw generic compound at the bottom.

**The funnel:**
```
Ingredient or question → Evidence → Products → Door
```

They arrive curious or confused. They leave informed and through a conversion link.
Supplifyed opens the door and gets out of the way.

### The Ingredient Page

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| What it is | Plain language definition. No jargon. |
| What the evidence says | Peer-reviewed research. Dose ranges. What was shown. What was not. |
| Evidence category | Strong / Moderate / Mixed / Limited |
| Common uses | Empirical list with sources |
| Dose context | What studies used vs what products typically contain |
| Products containing it | Three-tier product stack |
| Generic compound link | Raw ingredient. Unbranded. Lowest cost. |

### The Product Page

Every product gets the same page structure. Same sections. Same limits.
No brand gets more real estate than another. The framework is the equaliser.

| Section | Badge | Limit |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Manufacturer Label — verbatim packet text | LABEL | None — it is what it is |
| Official Specs — ingredients, doses, certs, RRP | MANUFACTURER | Full spec |
| Brand Marketing — submitted copy and media | BRAND MARKETING | Capped equally |
| Peer Reviewed — research at doses present | PEER REVIEWED | Sourced and linked |
| Press — published media coverage | PRESS | Sourced and dated |
| Instructional — usage guidance | INSTRUCTIONAL | Capped |

**The "who is saying it" principle:**
Every piece of content answers one question instantly — who produced this?
The user never has to wonder if they are being sold to.
Label text sits next to marketing copy. The gap between them, if there is one, is visible.
You said nothing. The documents speak.

## THE THREE-TIER SYSTEM

Every product category presented across three tiers. No ranking. Just market reality.

| Tier | The Product | Buyer Psychology |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| **ASPIRATION** | Highest spec. Premium. Most expensive. | They look at it. A lot. Validates the category. |
| **RATIONAL** | Most of the same features. Best seller band. | Where most conversions happen. The commission engine. |
| **ECONOMIC** | Accessible. Everyone has one. Entry price. | High volume. Low barrier. Catches rational dropout. |

The aspiration tier validates the category.
It makes the rational tier feel like a smart compromise, not a budget choice.
The economic tier catches everyone the rational tier loses.

You are not recommending any of them.
You are showing the market has three answers to the same problem.

### The Generic Ingredient Link

A fourth data point sits beneath all three tiers for supplements.
The raw compound. Potassium Citrate instead of the branded electrolyte blend.
This shows — without saying — that the branded product is largely a known compound with a markup and a logo.
Still empirical. Still no opinion. The link exists. The user decides.

| Devices / hardware | Brand official store + Amazon listing |

## MANUFACTURER SUBMISSIONS

Manufacturers — and only manufacturers — may submit an unlisted product.

**The model:**
They write the copy. They supply the specs. They provide certifications.
They give you the conversion link.
You compile it, format it, publish it inside the neutral framework.
They do the work. You take the commission. Free labour. Paying door.

### Submission Rules

- Manufacturers only. No resellers, distributors, or third parties.
- Must provide verified conversion link with affiliate structure.
- Content published within standard page framework. Same limits. Equal treatment.
- Brand marketing section capped. No brand receives more real estate than any other.
- Submissions cannot remove or alter the generic ingredient link. Non-negotiable.
- If product already in Best Sellers index, submission enriches the page. Does not replace.
- Platform publishes what is submitted. Does not validate manufacturer claims beyond source badging.

The sleeper. Near-zero marginal cost. Starts as a byproduct. Becomes primary at scale.

Why it is uniquely valuable: users on Supplifyed are not being sold to. Behaviour is uncontaminated.
Brands cannot get this signal from their own analytics — their site only sees decided buyers.

**Legal:** Aggregate and anonymised only. GDPR compliant by design. Transparent in privacy policy.
